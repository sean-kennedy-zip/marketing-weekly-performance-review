The steadiest channel in the portfolio, tracking near pro-rated pace in week 4. Volume is concentrated in two campaigns: the main demo request form (OG-Request-Demo-1036: 21 S1s) and Qualified Chat Lead Capture (SA-Evergreen: 8 S1s). Conversion rates are stable across all three stages vs. Q1 — INQ→SAL essentially flat at 64.8%, SAL→S0 at 41.2% (vs. 44.1% Q1, within tolerance), S0→S1 at 69.1% (vs. 73.0% Q1, within tolerance).

A note worth flagging: Aarin identified that customers signing up for product webinars (Contract Orchestration, P2P) are checking the demo-request box, generating Organic Demo credit for what are likely upsell conversations routed to AMs. As product-led motions grow, this attribution bleed will increase. The team should monitor whether these "customer demo requests" are being handled correctly downstream.

Within Q2, 29 open S0s exist (7 future, 17 past-due, 5 no-date). The 17 past-due and 5 no-date records suggest MDR follow-up gaps for recent Organic Demo SALs that deserve a disposition pass.

QTD: 6 S1s | Target: 20 | Pro-rated: ~5.5 | Pacing: 109.1% 🟢

Email is slightly ahead of pace and showing improved S0→S1 conversion (+14.7% relative vs. Q1, 68.8% vs. 60.0% 🟢). Aarin is planning a nurture restructure in June — repositioning MOFU content to run first to capture broader intent, then layering a new bottom-funnel sequence to drive more demo requests. The channel's performance will track with the Organic Content supply chain. Email and Organic Content should be analyzed together going forward — when new content lands, Email is typically the distribution engine that turns downloads into attributed S1s.
